# SKILL: Research Performance Hooks

## Purpose
Analyze high-engagement LinkedIn posts, articles, and ads in the B2B AI space. Break them down into their structural components to isolate what drives engagement for DACH decision-makers, filtering out irrelevant "hype" content.

## x10aix Content Standards
- **Anrede:** Always "du" on LinkedIn.
- **Tone:** Direct, declarative, thought-provoking, and pragmatic.
- **Format:** Max 2-3 emojis, no text walls.
- **Avoid:** Reposts, technical deep dives without a business hook, generic sales pitches, "AI Transformation" buzzwords.

## Analysis Framework
When evaluating a top-performing post:
1. **The Hook (First 2 Lines):** Deconstruct the opening. Is it a negative trigger (fear/risk), a surprising statistic, a contrarian opinion, or a relatable pain point?
2. **The Body (Structure):** How is the argument built? (Bulleted lists, storytelling, step-by-step guides).
3. **The Engagement Trigger (End):** How does the author prompt comments/likes? (e.g., asking for an opinion, offering a resource in exchange for a comment).
4. **Audience Alignment:** Is the engagement coming from the *right* audience (DACH Executives) or just AI enthusiasts/students?

## Output Structure
For each analyzed content piece, provide:
- **Content Deconstruction:** The exact Hook, Body structure, and CTA.
- **Why It Worked:** The psychological or structural reason for the high engagement.
- **Relevance to x10aix:** Fit with our executive, pragmatic positioning (Scale 1-10).
- **x10aix Adaptation:** Draft a 2-line Hook for x10aix inspired by this post, tailored to our specific services (Strategy, Knowledge Automation, Integration).
